# EU AI Act Requirements for AI Agent Systems

**Research Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Target System:** do-deal-relay (Autonomous Deal Discovery AI Agent)  
**Regulation:** EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)  
**Effective Date for High-Risk Systems:** August 2, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The EU AI Act establishes a risk-based approach to AI regulation. For autonomous AI agent systems like **do-deal-relay**, the classification depends on the specific use case and capabilities. Based on the system's function (autonomous deal discovery with web research and multi-agent coordination), this analysis provides compliance requirements across all risk categories.

### Risk Classification Assessment for do-deal-relay

| Category | Likely Classification | Rationale |
|----------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Primary Classification** | **Limited Risk / Minimal Risk** | Deal discovery and research automation without direct high-stakes decision-making |
| **If Enhanced with Profile Analysis** | **High-Risk** (Annex III - Point 4: Employment/Worker Management) | If used for screening candidates or making recruitment decisions |
| **If Used for Credit Assessment** | **High-Risk** (Annex III - Point 5: Credit Scoring) | If evaluating creditworthiness of individuals |

**Current Assessment:** As a deal discovery and research system, do-deal-relay likely falls under **Limited Risk** requiring transparency obligations (Article 50) rather than full high-risk compliance. However, providers should prepare for high-risk compliance if the system evolves.

## 1. Logging and Record-Keeping Requirements

### Article 12: Record-Keeping (High-Risk Systems)

**Key Requirements:**

1. **Automatic Recording Capability** (Article 12.1)
   - High-risk AI systems must technically allow for automatic recording of events (logs) over the system's entire lifetime
   - Must be built into the system design from the outset

2. **Traceability Requirements** (Article 12.2)
   Logging must enable recording of events relevant for:
   - Identifying situations that may result in risks or substantial modifications
   - Facilitating post-market monitoring (Article 72)
   - Monitoring operation of high-risk AI systems (Article 26(5))

3. **Minimum Logging Requirements for Biometric/Identification Systems** (Article 12.3)
   For systems referred to in point 1(a) of Annex III, logs must include:
   - **Period of each use:** Start date/time and end date/time of each use
   - **Reference database:** Database against which input data was checked
   - **Input data:** Data for which search led to a match
   - **Human verification:** Identification of natural persons involved in verification of results (Article 14(5))

### Article 19: Automatically Generated Logs (Provider Obligations)

- Providers must keep logs automatically generated by their high-risk AI systems when under their control
- Must maintain logs for appropriate period considering intended purpose
- **Minimum retention period:** 6 months
- Must comply with Union/national law on personal data protection (GDPR)

### Article 26(6): Deployer Log Retention

- Deployers must keep automatically generated logs for at least **6 months**
- Exception: If longer periods required by other Union/national law
- Financial institutions may maintain logs as part of internal governance documentation

## 2. Transparency Obligations

### Article 13: Transparency to Deployers (High-Risk Systems)

Providers must provide instructions for use including:

1. **Identity Information**
   - Provider contact details
   - Authorized representative (if applicable)

2. **System Characteristics** (Article 13.3(b))
   - Intended purpose
   - Level of accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity (Article 15)
   - Known/foreseeable circumstances affecting performance
   - Risks to health, safety, fundamental rights (Article 9(2))
   - Technical capabilities for explaining output
   - Performance regarding specific persons or groups
   - Input data specifications and training data information

3. **Change Documentation**
   - Pre-determined changes to system and performance at moment of initial conformity assessment

4. **Human Oversight Measures** (Article 13.3(d))
   - Technical measures to facilitate interpretation of outputs

5. **Operational Information**
   - Computational and hardware resources needed
   - Expected lifetime
   - Maintenance and care measures
   - Software update procedures

6. **Log Collection Mechanisms** (Article 13.3(f))
   - Description of mechanisms for deployers to properly collect, store, and interpret logs

### Article 50: Transparency Obligations for Certain AI Systems

**Applies to ALL AI systems interacting with natural persons:**

1. **AI Interaction Disclosure** (Article 50.1)
   - Users must be informed they are interacting with an AI system
   - Unless obvious from context
   - Must be clear and distinguishable at time of first interaction

2. **Synthetic Content Marking** (Article 50.2)
   - AI-generated audio, image, video, or text must be marked as artificially generated
   - Must be machine-readable and detectable
   - Technical solutions must be effective, interoperable, robust, reliable

3. **Emotion Recognition / Biometric Categorization** (Article 50.3)
   - Natural persons must be informed of system operation
   - Personal data processing must comply with GDPR

4. **Deepfake Disclosure** (Article 50.4)
   - Must disclose that content is artificially generated/manipulated
   - Exception: Artistic, creative, satirical works (disclosure limited to existence of generated content)
   - Text informing public on matters of public interest must be disclosed as AI-generated

5. **Timing and Accessibility** (Article 50.5)
   - Information provided at latest at time of first interaction/exposure
   - Must conform to applicable accessibility requirements

## 3. Risk Classification Analysis

### Article 6: Classification Rules for High-Risk AI Systems

**High-risk if:**
1. Used as safety component of product covered by Union harmonization legislation (Annex I) AND requires third-party conformity assessment; OR
2. Listed under Annex III use cases (unless exempted)

### Annex III: High-Risk Use Cases

**Potentially Relevant to do-deal-relay:**

| Point | Use Case | Relevance |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **4(a)** | Recruitment/selection AI | **HIGH** - If used to analyze/filter deal proposals involving individuals |
| **4(b)** | Work-related decisions | **MEDIUM** - If making decisions affecting partnerships |
| **5(a)** | Essential public services | **LOW** - Not directly applicable |
| **5(b)** | Credit scoring | **MEDIUM** - If evaluating creditworthiness |
| **6** | Law enforcement | **LOW** - Not applicable |
| **8(a)** | Justice administration | **LOW** - Not applicable |

### Article 6(3): Exemptions from High-Risk

AI systems in Annex III are **NOT** high-risk if they:
1. Perform narrow procedural task
2. Improve result of previously completed human activity
3. Detect decision-making patterns without replacing human assessment
4. Perform preparatory task to assessment

**Always HIGH-RISK if profiling individuals** (automated processing of personal data to assess aspects of person's life)

### Risk Classification Verdict for do-deal-relay

**Current System (Deal Discovery):**
- **Classification:** Limited Risk
- **Rationale:** Research automation without direct individual profiling or high-stakes decisions
- **Primary Obligation:** Transparency (Article 50)

**If Extended to Candidate Screening:**
- **Classification:** HIGH-RISK (Annex III, Point 4(a))
- **Obligations:** Full Chapter III compliance (Articles 8-17)

## 4. Data Governance Requirements

### Article 10: Data and Data Governance

**Applies to:** High-risk AI systems using training data

**Requirements:**

1. **Training, Validation, Testing Data Sets** (Article 10.2)
   Must implement data governance practices covering:
   - Design choices
   - Data collection processes and origin
   - Data preparation operations (annotation, labelling, cleaning, updating, enrichment, aggregation)
   - Assumptions about data measurement/representation
   - Assessment of availability, quantity, suitability of needed data sets
   - Examination of possible biases (health, safety, fundamental rights, discrimination)
   - Measures to detect, prevent, and mitigate biases
   - Identification of data gaps/shortcomings and how to address them

2. **Data Quality Standards** (Article 10.3)
   Data sets must be:
   - Relevant
   - Sufficiently representative
   - Free of errors (to best extent possible)
   - Complete in view of intended purpose
   - Have appropriate statistical properties

3. **Context-Specific Data** (Article 10.4)
   - Must consider geographical, contextual, behavioural, or functional setting

4. **Special Category Data Processing** (Article 10.5)
   May process special categories of personal data ONLY if:
   - Strictly necessary for bias detection and correction
   - Cannot be fulfilled by other data (including synthetic/anonymized)
   - Subject to technical limitations on re-use
   - State-of-the-art security and privacy-preserving measures (pseudonymization)
   - Strict controls and documentation of access
   - Not transmitted/transferable to other parties
   - Deleted once bias corrected or retention period ends (whichever first)
   - Records include reasons why processing was strictly necessary

## 5. Human Oversight Requirements

### Article 14: Human Oversight

**Key Requirements:**

1. **Design for Oversight** (Article 14.1)
   - Must be designed/developed to be effectively overseen by natural persons
   - Must include appropriate human-machine interface tools

2. **Oversight Objectives** (Article 14.2)
   - Prevent/minimize risks to health, safety, fundamental rights
   - Address risks persisting despite other requirements

3. **Oversight Measures** (Article 14.3)
   Must be commensurate with:
   - Risks involved
   - Level of autonomy
   - Context of use

   Implemented through:
   - Measures built into system by provider (when technically feasible)
   - Measures to be implemented by deployer

4. **Deployer Capabilities** (Article 14.4)
   System must enable overseers to:
   - Understand system capacities and limitations
   - Monitor operation and detect anomalies, dysfunctions, unexpected performance
   - Remain aware of automation bias (over-reliance on AI output)
   - Correctly interpret output using available tools/methods
   - Decide not to use system or disregard/override/reverse output
   - Intervene or interrupt system via 'stop' button or similar safe procedure

5. **Two-Person Verification** (Article 14.5)
   For biometric identification systems (Annex III, point 1(a)):
   - No action/decision based on identification unless separately verified by at least 2 natural persons
   - Persons must have necessary competence, training, and authority
   - Exception: Law enforcement, migration, border control, asylum (if Union/national law considers requirement disproportionate)

## 6. Technical Documentation Requirements

### Article 11: Technical Documentation

**Requirements:**

1. **Documentation Timing** (Article 11.1)
   - Must be drawn up BEFORE placing on market or putting into service
   - Must be kept up-to-date

2. **Documentation Purpose**
   - Demonstrate compliance with Chapter III requirements
   - Provide authorities necessary information in clear, comprehensive form

3. **Documentation Content**
   - Must contain elements set out in **Annex IV**
   - SMEs may provide elements in simplified manner (Commission will establish simplified form)

4. **Combined Documentation** (Article 11.2)
   - For AI systems related to products under Union harmonization legislation (Annex I, Section A)
   - Single set of technical documentation containing all required information

### Annex IV: Technical Documentation Elements

Minimum elements include:
1. General description of AI system
2. Detailed description of system architecture
3. Detailed description of training, validation, testing data
4. Description of quality management system
5. Detailed description of risk management system
6. Description of any change to system
7. List of harmonized standards applied
8. Copy of EU declaration of conformity
9. Detailed description of post-market monitoring plan

## 7. Conformity Assessment

### Article 43: Conformity Assessment Procedures

**For High-Risk Systems Listed in Point 1 of Annex III (Biometrics):**

**Option A - Internal Control** (Annex VI)
- If provider applied harmonized standards or common specifications
- Provider self-assesses compliance

**Option B - Notified Body Assessment** (Annex VII)
- Required when:
  - No harmonized standards exist
  - Provider has not applied standards
  - Standards published with restriction
  - For law enforcement, immigration, asylum authorities

**For High-Risk Systems in Points 2-8 of Annex III:**
- Internal control procedure (Annex VI) - no notified body required

### Article 47: EU Declaration of Conformity

Requirements:
- Draw up before placing on market/putting into service
- Keep up-to-date
- Must contain elements in **Annex V**
- Must be provided with system

### Article 48: CE Marking

Requirements:
- Affix CE marking before placing on market
- Must be visible, legible, indelible
- If not possible on system, on packaging/documentation

### Article 49: Registration

Requirements:
- Register high-risk AI systems in EU database before placing on market/putting into service
- Submit information per **Annex VIII**

## Implementation Recommendations for do-deal-relay

### Phase 1: Immediate (Pre-August 2025)

1. **Conduct Legal Assessment**
   - Confirm risk classification with legal counsel
   - Document use cases and boundaries

2. **Implement Basic Transparency**
   - Add AI disclosure to all user interfaces
   - Create system documentation
   - Publish provider contact information

3. **Establish Data Governance**
   - Document data sources and collection methods
   - Create data quality assessment procedures
   - Implement bias detection mechanisms

### Phase 2: Compliance Preparation (2025-2026)

1. **If Limited Risk:**
   - Maintain transparency measures
   - Document system capabilities/limitations
   - Prepare for potential classification changes

2. **If High-Risk:**
   - Implement full risk management system
   - Develop technical documentation per Annex IV
   - Establish quality management system
   - Build automatic logging infrastructure
   - Design human oversight interfaces
   - Plan conformity assessment procedure
   - Prepare EU declaration of conformity

### Phase 3: Ongoing Compliance

1. **Post-Market Monitoring** (Article 72)
   - Monitor system performance in production
   - Track identified risks and incidents
   - Implement continuous improvement

2. **Documentation Updates**
   - Keep technical documentation current
   - Document all changes to system
   - Maintain version control

3. **Audit Trail Maintenance**
   - Preserve logs for required periods
   - Ensure log integrity and security
   - Enable authority access when required
